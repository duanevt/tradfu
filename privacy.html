PRIVACY POLICY

Tradfu Holdings Pty Ltd (“Tradfu”)

Last updated: August 31, 2016

We are committed to protecting your privacy as Tradfu Holdings Pty Ltd (“Tradfu”) users. We use the personal information we collect from our mobile applications, websites and other online products and services (collectively, the “Services”) to maximise the value we provide to you. This policy applies to all Tradfu users and explains how we collect, use, and safeguard your personal information in conformance with Internet Privacy Standards.

If you have questions or concerns regarding this statement, you should first contact us at support@tradfu.com.au.

Collection of Information

Depending on the specific circumstances of your Tradfu usage, we may collect and hold a range of information about you. You may provide basic contact information such as your name, phone number, address and email address to allow us to send information (such as an e-newsletter) to you. If you are purchasing Services from Tradfu, you may provide financial information such as credit card details.

This is not an exhaustive list. For example, we may also collect additional information at other times, including but not limited to, when you provide feedback, respond to a survey or communicate with us in relation to queries.

How we collect your information

In order to use the Tradfu website, we may require information from you in order to provide the best service possible.

All correspondence may also be collected and stored, particularly in regard to sales, support and accounts, including email.

Any information collected by Tradfu is collected via correspondence from you or your company. This may be via the telephone, email, mail, fax or directly through our website.

We may collect your information in a number of ways, including:

  • directly from you (for example, when you register your details with us; subscribe to publications through this website; participate in promotions; competitions or surveys that we run; or contact us with a query or request);
  • directly from you on behalf of another person that you referred to Tradfu, Tradfu requires that you obtain their permission first and inform that person of this occurrence and provide them with this Privacy Policy;
  • from publicly available sources of information.

How we hold your information

The security of your personal information is important to us. We may store your information in hard copy or electronic format, in storage facilities that we own and operate ourselves, or that are owned and operated by our service providers. We take reasonable steps to maintain the security of your information and to protect it from unauthorised disclosures.

When you enter sensitive information (such as credit card numbers) on our website, we encrypt that information using secure socket layer technology (SSL). When Credit Card details are collected, we simply pass them on in order to be processed as required. We never permanently store complete Credit Card details.

We follow generally accepted industry standards to protect the personal information submitted to us, both during transmission and once we receive it.

If you have any questions about security on our Website, you can email us at support@tradfu.com.

While we take these steps to maintain the security of your information, you should be aware of the many information security risks that exist today and take appropriate care to help safeguard your information.

Access to Collected Information

If your personally identifiable information changes, or if you no longer desire our service, you may correct, update, delete or deactivate it by emailing us at support@tradfu.com

Use of Collected Information

Any details collected from Tradfu customers is required in order to provide you with our products and/or services, and a high level of customer service.

Correspondence is recorded in order to provide service references, and to assist in our staff development.

We may use your information for a range of different purposes but not limited to:

  • to provide information to you (e.g. e-newsletters, promotional materials), to enhance your experience on the website, or to provide you with better customer service;
  • to gain an understanding of your information needs, to perform market research and analysis, and to improve or develop the content on the website;
  • to monitor the website’s use, quality and performance, and to operate, maintain, develop, test and upgrade our systems, infrastructure or business offerings.

How we use your information for direct marketing

We may use personal information collected from you for the purpose of providing you with direct marketing material and information. If you wish to cease receiving any such information you can opt out by email at support@tradfu.com and we will action your request as soon as practicable.

Payment Security

Tradfu Holdings Pty Ltd (“Tradfu”) uses the eWAY Payment Gateway for its online credit card transactions. eWAY processes online credit card transactions for merchants, providing a safe and secure means of collecting payments via the Internet. All online credit card transactions performed on this site using the eWAY gateway are secured payments.

  • Payments are fully automated with an immediate response.
  • Your complete credit card number cannot be viewed by Tradfu or any outside party. - All transactions are performed under 128 Bit SSL Certificate.
  • All transaction data is encrypted for storage within eWAY’s bank-grade data centre, further protecting your credit card data.
  • eWAY at no time touches your funds; all monies are directly transferred from your credit card to the merchant account held by Tradfu.

For more information about eWAY and online credit card payments, please visit www.eWAY.com.au.

Disclosure of your information

Tradfu may at its discretion use other third parties to provide essential services on our site or for our business processes. We may share your details as necessary for the third party to provide that service

These third parties are prohibited from using your personally identifiable information for any other purpose.

Tradfu does not share any information with third parties for any unknown or unrelated uses. We will not knowingly disclose your personal information to third parties except to:

  • law enforcement and national security agencies, and other government and regulatory authorities as required or authorised by law;
  • our professional advisers, such as our legendary lawyers and number crunching accountants, where required for them to advise us;
  • a purchaser or prospective purchaser for the purposes of facilitating or implementing a transfer/sale of all or part of our assets or business. In such circumstances, we would only disclose your personal information in good faith.

We will not disclose your personal information to anyone outside Australia.

Legal

We reserve the right to disclose your personally identifiable information as required by law and when we believe that disclosure is necessary to protect our rights and/or comply with a judicial proceeding, court order, or legal process served on our Website.

How you may complain about a breach of the Australian Privacy Principles

You may also use those contact details to notify us of any privacy complaint you have against us, including if you think that we have failed to comply with the Australian Privacy Principles (APP) or any binding APP code that has been registered under the Privacy Act. We are committed to acknowledging your complaint in a prompt manner and will give you an estimated timeframe for when we will respond to your complaint.

Whilst we sincerely hope that we will be able to resolve any complaints you may have without the involvement of third parties, you may also be able to lodge a complaint with a relevant regulator such as the Australian Information Commissioner (www.oaic.gov.au) or the Telecommunications Industry Ombudsman (www.tio.com.au).

Change in Privacy Policy

As we are committed to ensuring that our privacy policy remains current, this policy is subject to change. We may modify this policy at any time, in our sole discretion, and all modifications will be effective immediately upon our publication of the updated Privacy Policy on this website.
